About this dashboard

Approach, design decisions, and roadmap

Approach

A single-page summary of competitor activity, AI-in-banking news, and US banking-regulator updates. Refreshed automatically once per day at 07:00 PT.

Architecture: a Python job pulls items from multiple public sources, deduplicates across them, tags each item by competitor and category, and writes the result as JSON files in the repository. A static HTML page reads those files directly. No backend services or databases are involved. Operating cost is $0 per month.

Editorial principle: precision over recall. Tagging and filtering rules are tuned to minimize false positives in the Competitor Intel tab, accepting that occasional marginal items may be missed.

Design decisions

Four tabs: three content, one personal

Competitor Intel, AI News, and Regulatory are the content tabs, populated by the daily job. CEO Brief stores items starred from any tab and includes a share-link option and a print view for 1:1 preparation.

Regex-validated competitor tagging

Google News and Hacker News searches both use fuzzy matching by default. A search for "Eltropy" can return articles about "Entropy"; a search for "Posh" can return hotel coverage. Each candidate item is checked against a narrow per-competitor regular expression before appearing in Competitor Intel. Items that fail the check are dropped from the feed entirely rather than moved to AI News.

Case-sensitive matching for ambiguous names

"born digital" lowercase is a common English phrase (digital natives). "Born Digital" Title Case is the company. The Born Digital matcher is case-sensitive. Glia and Posh use similar context-based guards.

Regulatory tab filters foreign-policy content

CFPB, OCC, NCUA, FDIC, Federal Reserve, and Treasury press feeds publish items on sanctions, tariffs, and international affairs alongside domestic financial regulation. A keyword filter excludes the international items and keeps only US domestic regulatory news.

Per-source resilience

Each source is wrapped in error handling so a single broken feed does not prevent the rest of the run from completing. The footer of every tab shows live source health (number of sources OK out of total).

Sources not currently pulled, and what enabling each requires

Twitter / X

The X API now starts at approximately $100 per month for the basic read-only tier. Free scraping is rate-limited and IP-blocked from automated environments. No reliable free path exists.

To enable: subscribe to the X basic API tier, or wait for free read access to return.

Bluesky

Previously attempted and removed. The public search endpoint now requires authentication. Even with credentials, signal-to-noise on competitor keywords was low — short informal posts containing words such as "posh" or "active AI" frequently passed the relevance filter.

To enable: two options. (a) curate a list of specific Bluesky accounts and pull only their posts. (b) add an LLM-based relevance filter before posts enter the feed, at approximately $1 per month at current volume.

Reddit

Reddit's own API blocks the IP range used by GitHub Actions. The dashboard currently routes Reddit content through the pullpush.io community archive, which adds delay and depends on a third party.

To harden: register a Reddit OAuth application (no cost) and store two additional secrets.

Competitor blogs

Most competitor blogs are not on standardized RSS paths. Omilia's blog is currently pulled. The remaining competitors require their RSS URL to be located manually and added to the source list.

To fill in: for each remaining competitor (Eltropy, Kasisto, Posh, Glia, Gridspace, Born Digital), identify the actual blog RSS URL and add it to COMPETITOR_BLOG_FEEDS.

Roadmap

  1. AI daily brief at the top. An LLM summarization step generates a short "today's three things" headline section. Estimated cost: $1–2 per month.
  2. Email or Slack digest at 07:00. Same content delivered to inbox or channel before standup.
  3. Customer-win tracker. Pattern match for "[Competitor] signs [Bank or Credit Union]" across sources, surfaced as its own sub-tab.
  4. Hiring velocity tracker. Periodic scrape of competitor /careers pages. Conversational-AI engineering postings can indicate investment direction.
  5. Earnings-call mentions. Bank and credit union earnings transcripts often reference vendor partners. Mentions of the tracked competitor set could be surfaced via Seeking Alpha or Capital IQ.
  6. Sentiment over time. Tone of social and news mentions per competitor, tracked as a chart, for momentum monitoring.
