About this dashboard

Approach, design decisions & roadmap

A working brief on why the dashboard exists, the choices behind what it does (and doesn't) pull, and the next moves on the table.

Approach

The point is to compress an entire morning of competitive scanning into one page that loads in under a second. Every day at 07:00 PT a Python job pulls fresh signal from a dozen sources, dedupes across them, tags each item with the competitor and category it belongs to, and commits the result as JSON. The site reads that JSON — no backend, no database, no servers to babysit. Total operating cost is zero dollars a month.

Signal beats volume. We'd rather show twelve real competitor items than thirty with eighteen false positives, so source coverage and tagging rules are tuned aggressively against noise — even at the cost of occasionally missing a marginal item.

Design decisions

Two tabs of content, one tab of personal saves

Competitor Intel and AI News are the consumption surfaces; Regulatory adds banking-policy signal that almost no competitor watches; CEO Brief is your personal staging area — star anything from any tab and it lands there with a shareable link and a print view for 1:1 prep.

Trust the regex, not the search

Google News and HN search both fuzzy-match aggressively. A query for "Eltropy" returns articles about "Entropy"; a query for "Posh" returns hotel reviews. Every item is verified against a narrow per-competitor regex before it's allowed onto the Competitor Intel tab — items that fail the check are dropped entirely, not demoted to AI News.

Case-sensitive matching for ambiguous names

"born digital" lowercase is a common phrase meaning digital natives. "Born Digital" Title Case is the company. The Born Digital matcher is case-sensitive on purpose; Glia and Posh have similar guards baked in.

Regulatory tab filters foreign policy

CFPB, OCC, NCUA, FDIC, Fed and Treasury press feeds technically include sanctions, tariffs, and Iran/Russia coverage. None of that is useful for a banking-AI strategy conversation, so we filter those keywords out and keep only domestic financial regulation.

Per-source resilience

Every source is wrapped so a single broken feed never kills the whole run. The footer of every tab shows live source health; if you see the failed count creep up, that's the signal something needs tightening.

Sources we deliberately don't pull — and what it would take

Twitter / X

X's API now starts at ~$100/month for the basic read-only tier, and free scraping is rate-limited and IP-blocked. Without paid access there's no reliable way to ingest X content.

How to enable: sponsor a $100/mo basic API seat or wait for X to restore free read access. Would re-introduce live analyst conversation and AI-Twitter chatter that doesn't appear anywhere else.

Bluesky

Attempted and pulled. The public search endpoint now requires auth, and even with credentials the signal-to-noise on competitor names was poor — short informal posts containing words like "posh" or "active AI" leaked through every filter.

How to enable: two paths. (a) curate a Bluesky list of high-signal accounts (banking AI analysts, vendor engineers) and pull only their posts — trades breadth for precision. (b) add an LLM-based semantic-relevance filter before posts enter the feed (~$1/month at current volume).

Reddit

Reddit blocks our automation's IPs at the API layer. We currently route via the community-run pullpush.io archive — works but adds delay and depends on a third party staying up.

How to harden: move to Reddit's own OAuth flow — free, requires registering an app and storing two more secrets. Worth it if pullpush gets flaky.

Competitor blogs

Most competitor blogs aren't on standardized RSS paths. Today we pull from Omilia; the rest need their actual feed URL probed and added.

How to fill in: for each remaining competitor (Eltropy, Kasisto, Posh, Glia, Gridspace, Born Digital), find the real blog RSS and add it to COMPETITOR_BLOG_FEEDS.

Roadmap — what to build next

  1. AI daily brief at the top. Claude reads the day's items and writes "3 things you need to know" in plain English. ~$1–2/month in API calls. Highest leverage by a mile — turns a feed into a briefing.
  2. Email or Slack digest at 07:00. Same content auto-delivered before standup — you scan it before the dashboard ever opens.
  3. Customer-win tracker. Dedicated pattern match for "[Competitor] signs [Bank/CU]" across all sources, surfaced as its own sub-tab.
  4. Hiring velocity tracker. Scrape competitor /careers pages. New conversational-AI engineer postings are a leading indicator of where they're investing.
  5. Earnings-call mentions. Bank and CU earnings transcripts often name vendor partners. Surface every mention of our competitor set via Seeking Alpha / Capital IQ.
  6. Sentiment over time. Track tone of social/news mentions per competitor as a small chart — useful for spotting momentum shifts before they break.
